Claim Safety

Website Information Is Not a Final Recommendation

The information on this website is for general homeowner education and consultation planning. It does not replace an exterior inspection, a written proposal, manufacturer product data, safety data sheets, permit review, or final scope documents.

  • No website statement creates a contract, warranty, financing offer, insurance opinion, engineering opinion, or final product recommendation.
  • All performance expectations depend on surface condition, preparation, product selection, application conditions, maintenance, exposure, and written product documentation.
  • PrimeCoat Florida avoids promises about specific utility-bill outcomes, indoor temperature changes, mold removal, or one-size-fits-all compatibility.
  • Photos, renderings, gallery visuals, and video spaces may be representative until replaced with labeled PrimeCoat project assets.

Products & Compatibility

Product Selection and Surface Compatibility

Coating and finish products are selected only after consultation, surface review, and consideration of current manufacturer documentation. PrimeCoat Florida may update product recommendations as approved coating lines, availability, specifications, and project requirements change.

  • Product availability, colors, sheens, specifications, application requirements, and documentation may change.
  • Coatings, primers, conditioners, sealants, paints, stains, or other finish products are selected only after surface review.
  • Possible surfaces may include stucco, concrete, masonry, brick, CMU/block, cement board, compatible previously coated surfaces, trim, and exterior wood details, depending on condition and manufacturer guidance.
  • Some surfaces may need cleaning, repair, pH or moisture review, primer, conditioner, sealant, specialty preparation, or may not be a fit for the requested system.
  • Homeowners should review current product data sheets, safety data sheets, labels, and written proposal documents before approving work.

Warranty Terms

Warranty Language Depends on the Final Written Scope

Any warranty coverage depends on the selected products, manufacturer documentation, surface condition, preparation, application, exclusions, homeowner responsibilities, and final written project documents.

  • This website does not create additional warranty coverage beyond written manufacturer or project documents.
  • Warranty terms should be reviewed before work begins and should identify what is covered, what is excluded, who provides coverage, and how claims are handled.
  • Existing damage, trapped moisture, structural movement, substrate failure, previous coating failure, water intrusion, or excluded conditions may affect coverage.

Financing Notice

Financing Options May Be Available for Qualified Homeowners

PrimeCoat Florida is not a lender. Financing, if available, is offered by third-party providers and is subject to credit approval, provider terms, identity verification, project scope, and availability.

  • Homeowners should compare financing terms, cash pricing, total cost, fees, repayment obligations, and cancellation rights before signing.
  • PrimeCoat Florida does not provide tax, credit, or financial advice.

Licensing, Permits & Scope

Final Business Details Must Be Confirmed in Writing

Applicable Florida contractor license information, insurance information, permit responsibility, and scope boundaries should be confirmed before regulated contracting services are advertised, contracted, or performed.

  • Do not rely on this website alone to determine whether a project needs a permit, licensed trade, engineer, inspector, remediation specialist, or separate contractor.
  • Final written proposals should identify scope, exclusions, access requirements, preparation, repairs, products, payment terms, schedule expectations, and cancellation rights that apply.
  • When Florida home solicitation or home improvement finance rules apply, final documents should include the required cancellation notice and timing.
  • If a contractor license number is required for the final service scope, it should be displayed in applicable advertising, proposals, and contracts before launch or use.
  • PrimeCoat Florida does not provide structural engineering, waterproofing remediation design, mold remediation, roofing, public adjusting, or insurance claim adjusting unless separately licensed and stated in writing.
